1. Astronomers are studying a star with a Teff of approximately 6000 K. ... Which lines are the most sensitive to surface gravity for the astronomers to consider?
   Answer: C) El2 II (singly ionized)At Teff ≈ 6000 K (roughly solar-like, G-type star), neutral lines (I) of metals are generally strong, but singly ionized lines (II) — especially for elements that are mostly ionized in the atmosphere — show stronger pressure broadening (Stark effect, wing sensitivity) and gravity dependence. This is because ionized species are more sensitive to changes in electron pressure and atmospheric density (which correlates with log g). Neutral lines (mostly for El1) are less gravity-sensitive in this regime, while the minority neutral lines of mostly-ionized El2 (El2 I) are weak and less useful. Classic examples include ionized metal lines (e.g., Fe II, Ca II) used alongside or instead of neutrals for gravity determination in cool stars.

2. Quantum Mechanics Methylcyclopentadiene ... How many chemically distinct isomers make up the final product (not counting stereoisomers)?
   Answer: D) 4Methylcyclopentadiene exists as a fluxional mixture of three main isomers (1-methyl, 2-methyl, and 5-methyl-1,3-cyclopentadiene; the 5-methyl is minor/tautomeric but contributes via rapid sigmatropic shifts). The amine-catalyzed (pyrrolidine) reaction with methyl isoamyl ketone (an unsymmetrical ketone) is a Knoevenagel-type condensation to form fulvene derivatives (bright yellow, cross-conjugated, loss of water). The fulvene double bond forms at the exocyclic position from the ketone carbonyl and the active methylene of the cyclopentadiene.Due to the unsymmetrical ketone (methyl vs isoamyl = 3-methylbutyl), each fulvene has distinct substitution: the exocyclic =C(R1)(R2) where R1 = methyl, R2 = isoamyl (or vice versa, but the ketone directs regiochemistry toward the less hindered or thermodynamic product). However, the key is the position of the methyl group on the cyclopentadiene ring in the fulvene.The three main methylcyclopentadiene tautomers lead to three distinct fulvene regioisomers (methyl at positions equivalent to 1-, 2-, or 3- in the fulvene numbering, but due to symmetry in fulvene, 1- and 3- are equivalent in some cases, though not here). In practice, the reaction yields a mixture dominated by the two major tautomers (1-methyl and 2-methyl), but literature and problem context indicate three fulvene isomers from the three CPD tautomers.These fulvenes then undergo Diels-Alder reaction with ethyl acrylate (as the dienophile) in 1:1 ratio. Fulvenes act as dienes in inverse-electron-demand or normal Diels-Alder with electron-poor dienophiles like acrylates, adding across the 2,5-positions of the fulvene (exocyclic double bond + ring diene system), giving adducts where the yellow color disappears (loss of extended conjugation).Each fulvene regioisomer reacts to give one distinct constitutional isomer of the adduct (the methyl group position on the norbornene-like bicyclic system differs). The reaction is regioselective due to the unsymmetrical fulvene, but since the fulvene mixture has different methyl positions, and the ketone asymmetry is fixed, there are 4 chemically distinct isomers in the final product mixture (accounting for the dominant contributions from the CPD tautomers and the unsymmetrical ketone leading to distinguishable substitution patterns in the adducts; stereoisomers like endo/exo are excluded).
